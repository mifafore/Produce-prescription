Diet-related chronic diseases (DRCDs) like obesity, diabetes, and heart disease cause significant disease burden, much of which can be prevented through health behavior changes. However, complex social and environmental factors like food insecurity and lack of access contribute to poor health outcomes. Multilevel, community-engaged interventions are especially needed to address health disparities. Prairie View A&M University (PVAMU) is located in Waller County, Texas, where 19% of residents were food insecure in 2016. As a rural food desert, many residents lack access to healthy, affordable food options despite the small land area. This is defined as having to travel half a mile in urban areas or 10+ miles in rural areas to obtain food. Those without private transportation may have to walk even farther.

To address campus food insecurity, PVAMU resources include a Food Pantry, Student-led Community Garden, Panther Meal Share Program, and the newly established Integrated Food Security Research Center. Still, developing and evaluating interventions that target the social and environmental drivers of health in underserved communities remains an urgent need.
