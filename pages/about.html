About Us

Our group of researchers at the Agriculture and Nutrition department aims to conduct an in-depth needs assessment of diet-related chronic diseases (DRCDs) and food insecurity issues among Prairie View A&M University (PVAMU) students and the surrounding community. By surveying students and locals, we will determine: Prevalence of DRCDs like obesity, diabetes, and heart disease on campus and in the community Rates of food insecurity among the student body Awareness and use of existing campus resources addressing food access issues Gaps in nutrition knowledge and barriers to healthy eating habits Available food outlets and places to exercise within 10 miles of PVAMU

Additionally, through interviews and focus groups we will gather insights into: Local perceptions, experiences, and needs related to DRCDs and food insecurity Barriers to healthy eating and purchasing quality food options on campus Overcoming obstacles and improving the campus food environment Food preferences, purchasing strategies, and interactions with the food environment Our needs assessment aims to deeply understand these issues in order to tailor appropriate interventions that maximize use of available resources to achieve results. Findings will guide our initiatives to promote healthy lifestyles and health equity within this underserved community.
